funderburkjim :

@Shalu411 Here's an explanation for the specific case. 001

Just look at the line with the arrow: `001 akzayawrtIyA -> (akzayatftIyA)`

The word up for correction is the word to the left of the arrow: `akzayawrtIyA `

The word (or words) to the right of the arrow are suggestions as to what the correct spelling *might* be.

You would look at the scanned image using the active html page [here](http://funderburkjim.github.io/fuzzywork/pui-fuzzyalpha.html):
![image](https://cloud.githubusercontent.com/assets/6393033/7263836/05861376-e853-11e4-9ad9-42268affb519.png)
which leads you to
![image](https://cloud.githubusercontent.com/assets/6393033/7263860/2c730890-e853-11e4-9d7e-bf89c2026bf6.png)


In this case even though the text has retroflect 't' that is clearly wrong (we could say its a print error or
a scan error). In this instance we agree with the suggestion . So in your local copy of pui-fuzzyalpha.txt
you would change that line with the arrow from

```
001 akzayawrtIyA -> (akzayatftIyA)
```
to
```
001 akzayawrtIyA -> akzayatftIyA : (akzayatftIyA)
```
I.e. the corrected spelling goes to the right of the arrow AND a 'Colon' follows the correction to separate it
from suggestions.

That's it.

Since Dhaval is working on PUI you don't need to. But the same principles apply to PGN in issue 103
which I hope you will try.

Hope this helps some.

gasyoun :

1. `How does one pronounce ळ ?`
As ḷ is instead of ḍ same is the pronunciation (and ḷh instead of ḍh). Pali grammarians (p. 40 Pali Language 1965) note that pronunciation of ऌ gets mixed with ल sometimes. In IAST a mix happens.

2. `Is ळ really part of Sanskrit alphabet? If so why is it absent from so many listings of the alphabet.`
There is no such thing as Sanskrit alphabet it's a fiction. For the need of Vedic texts and Marathi ळ is used. Sanskrit is a language. Devanagari is a writing syllabary. It's one of the alphabets widely used nowadays similar to Bengali characters used just 200 years ago.

3. `If so is the above omniglot display correct in its placement in alphabet?`
Yes and no. Omniglot's creator thinks I'm an authority (see http://www.omniglot.com/blog/?p=682 and http://www.omniglot.com/blog/?p=621) and I as an authority can say that it is positioned there only because there was some free place no scientific background. It should be in a footnote.

4. `Is there a 'standard' IAST representation? If so does the standard IAST representation of SLP1 'x' differ?`
See p. 12 of http://shashir.autodidactus.org/shashir_umich/dl/rapportdetranscription.pdf - IAST always had only ḷ but the mixing was always there (see a whole paragraph on it). I hardly understand how it can differ from SLP1 even theoretically because SLP1 is a pure abstraction that has nothing to do with phonetics diacritics of tradition.
