Let me clarify the position of Sanskrit grammar on the anusvAra issue.

A. There are two sUtras which mandate anusvAra in first place.
मोऽनुस्वारः and नश्चापदान्तस्य झलि.
Let me explain them from coding perspective for a dictionary.
A1. मोऽनुस्वारः -> When 'm' is at the end of the first member of a samAsa (compound) and is followed by any of consonant (kKgGNcCjJYwWqQRtTdDnpPbBmyrlvSzsh) it gets converted to anusvAra 'M'.
A2. नश्चापदान्तस्य झलि -> When 'n' is not at the end of a member of compound and is followed by any of these 'kKgGcCjJwWqQtTdDpPbBSzsh' it gets converted to anusvAra 'M'.
for our example 'idam+kArya' -> 'idaM+kArya'.

B. After conversion to 'M' from either 'm' or 'n' we check whether they are at the end of a pada or not (for dictionary purposes we check whether they are at the end of first member of a samAsa (compound) or not ).
e.g. 'idaM+kAraya' - here M is at the end of the first member of samAsa.

B1. If yes - अनुस्वारस्य ययि परसवर्णः and वा पदान्तस्य will apply. (optional forms)
B2. If no - अनुस्वारस्य ययि परसवर्णः will apply (mandatory).

If it is at the end of first member of a samAsa e.g. idaM+kArya it gets two optional forms 'idaMkArya' and 'idaNkArya'.
If it is not at the end of first member of a samAsa - it gets only mandatory form with conversion. e.g. idam+tana -> Here tana is a suffix and the word is not a samAsa. Therefore it is mandatorily to be converted to 'idantana'.

So
take home message is -
**If we don't want to enter into whether it is a samAsa or not - we can safely take the converted form
'idaNkArya' and 'idantana'.**
This way we won't be wrong any how.
